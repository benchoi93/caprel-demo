Congestion-Aware GFlowNet Sampling — Real Trained TB-GFN

2D 16×16 HyperGrid with asymmetric reward (one R=10 corner, four R=1.0–1.5 modes). Side-by-side: standard sampling vs. capacity-relative (CapRel) sampling. Forward pass uses the actual MLP weights from a TB-GFN trained for 4,000 steps in PyTorch. Companion to Paper 3.

Standard sampling (policy only)

Heatmap = visit counts. Star size ∝ reward; filled when discovered. Red traces = trajectories that terminated at a mode; gray traces with ✕ = "wasted" trajectories where the policy chose stop at a non-mode state. The trained policy collapses onto the dominant R=10 corner.

Capacity-relative sampling (CapRel)

Penalty: φ = λ · max(0, (c/(n·p))β − tβ). When the empirical visit ratio of a child action exceeds threshold t, probability is reallocated to under-visited siblings.

Modes discovered over time

x-axis = trajectories drawn; y-axis = unique modes found (out of 5). Red line = CapRel, blue line = standard. CapRel should climb faster and reach higher plateau; standard typically plateaus at 1–2 modes.

Is P(mode) ∝ R? Sampled distribution vs reward-proportional target

Each cluster of three bars is one mode. Gray = target (Ri/Σ Rj); blue = standard's empirical fraction; red = CapRel's. The closer blue/red are to gray, the better the sampler matches P ∝ R. Fractions are computed among mode-terminating trajectories.
